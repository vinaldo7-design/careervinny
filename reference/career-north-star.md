# Career North Star

## The one sentence
I want frontier AI-domain *strategy* work, delivered inside a prestige platform,
on a track that leads to people leadership — with the bleeding-edge work
front-loaded now because it's the perishable asset, and the ladder optimised for
because it's where status compounds.

## What I actually want, and why (the reasoning, not just the conclusion)

**Prestige is near-mandatory, and it's terminal not instrumental.** When forced
to choose, I take the recognised name over the founder's ownership and feel no
regret about the ownership I gave up. Status and trajectory are things I value
for their own sake, not as a means to something else. This is the stable signal —
it held across every reframing. The prestige requirement does not yield to more
frontier: I want frontier work *at* a prestige firm, not frontier work instead of
one. Frontier labs (Anthropic, OpenAI, and peers) count as prestige — the name
carries even though they're young, and they're where the cutting-edge strategy
work concentrates.

**Ownership of the *outcome* repels me; ownership of *people and craft* is the
goal.** I don't want to be the person who owns whether the bet works — the
founder's load, the ambiguous-outcome exposure. I do want to lead a team within a
structure someone above me set: develop them, be accountable for their work, but
not carry whether the company's whole direction was right. Those are different
kinds of ownership and only the second is mine. This is why I want a *legible
management ladder* — defined rungs into leadership — not just any prestigious
seat. A flat prestigious firm is worth less to me than a structured one.

**My judgment has to count — that's the non-negotiable texture.** The thing I'd
refuse is being execution capacity: deck-production labour, a puppet for a senior
exec, my intelligence treated as throughput. The autonomy I want is
autonomy-of-judgment (trusted to think, ideas register in the room), not
autonomy-of-ownership (running my own thing). Available inside a prestige
structure at the right firms.

**Frontier means frontier-of-the-strategy-conversation, not
frontier-of-the-codebase.** Cutting-edge tech-domain strategy work that my CS+DS
+Oxford-DipAI profile qualifies me for. NOT proximity to the model as an engineer.
I left software engineering and I'm not going back. Technical IC debugging drains
me; I can code if needed but I don't want to. An FDE writing production code is
"close to frontier tech" and still wrong for me — it's not strategy and it's the
grind I left.

**Intensity is workload-and-peer-bar, not responsibility-stretch.** "HPE was too
easy" means I want to be worked hard and surrounded by people sharper than me —
intellectually stretched inside a structure. It does NOT mean I want the lonely
stretch of being the person the unsolved problem lands on with no one above me.
Step-up over responsibility-stretch; intellectual stretch welcome; peer-bar beats
comfort. A demanding role where I'm the ninth-smartest in the room beats a
comfortable one where I'm the smartest.

**Travel is wanted, not tolerated.** Five years working from home with no travel
on the company dime. I want road-warrior intensity — it's a plus, not a cost.

**Ownership and innovation live in my own projects.** Mini Vinny, the Substack,
the building — that's where the originating drive is satisfied. The day job is for
prestige, money, security, trajectory, and frontier-strategy texture. Separating
these is what resolves the apparent contradiction between "I want prestige and
money" and "I want autonomy for innovation." Both are true; they live in different
rooms.

## The trajectory (this is a staircase, not a step)
I'm optimising the staircase, not the first step. At 27 with ~5 years in, the
**perishable asset is frontier exposure** — bleeding-edge AI strategy experience
compounds and has a shelf life (frontier-2026 is table-stakes-2029). The
**deferrable asset is the ladder** — I can climb from any good platform later, and
I'm honestly unsure of my odds of reaching leadership right now, so I won't
over-stake on it prematurely. Therefore: **when frontier and ladder genuinely
conflict, frontier wins** — but only *among already-prestige options*. I never
leave the prestige set to get more frontier. Player-coach now (craft + small
team), shed the craft as I climb.

The 41-year-old test that locked this: a terminal-IC oracle seat — top individual,
never owns an organisation, watches peers pass into leadership — reads to me as
*having been passed*, not as freedom. That's why the ladder stays load-bearing
even though I front-load frontier.

## My highest-leverage edge
**ESG × AI strategy.** ESG-assurance depth + AI building + Oxford AI strategy =
a rare, top-1% intersection. Low volume, high conversion. If I can land a
high-paying role at a prestige firm through this route, I exploit it hard. It must
not get buried under flashier generic-AI roles — it carries an explicit bonus.

## What this rules out (negative space — the things that repel me)
- Pure individual-contributor tracks, however shiny or well-paid (the oracle trap).
- Technical IC / engineering / production-code roles, including FDE-as-coding-job.
- Founder-load / outcome-ownership / running-whether-the-bet-works.
- Deck-production labour where my judgment doesn't register (the puppet role).
- Prestigious-but-dull: cost-takeout, rebadged digital transformation, back-office
  strategy at a great name. The name is necessary but not sufficient — dull
  frontier-free work at a top firm still fails.
- Sub-Series-C startups (no legible ladder, outcome-ownership bleeds in, weaker
  visa sponsorship). Prestige is near-mandatory; small does not clear it.

## Open / to revisit as I learn
- Whether my odds at the leadership ladder firm up enough to re-weight it upward.
- Whether the ESG×AI bonus is pulling real high-conversion roles or just niche
  noise — tune from the lessons loop.
- Industry remains open.
